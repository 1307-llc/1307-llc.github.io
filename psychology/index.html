The Astonishing Hypothesis: “You, your joys and your sorrows, your memories and your ambitions, your sense of personal identity and free will, are in fact no more than the behavior of a vast assembly of nerve cells and their associated molecules.”

Dualism: a common opinion

  • Descartes, “Are humans merely physical machines, merely physical things? No.” “Nonhuman animals are ‘Beast Machines’, merely robots; but people are different, we are immaterial souls that possess physical bodies.”
  • Humans are not limited to reflective actions, like machines; we are capable of coordinated creative spontaneous things. Language, etc.
  • One could doubt the existence of one’s body, like a madman might believe they have extra limbs. “How can I believe I am not crazy? How do I know I am not dreaming right now?” One cannot doubt that one is thinking.
  • “I knew that I was a substance, the whole essence or nature of which is to think, and that for its existence there is no need of any place, nor does it depend on any material thing. That is to say the soul by which I am, what I am, is entirely distinct from body.”
  • In our language, we talk about “my brain” as if we are somehow seperate from them.
  • Common sence indicates that somebody can be the same person even if their body has gone under radical changes.
  • Multiple personality disorder: perhaps many souls in one body and fighting for control?
