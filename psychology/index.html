1：大脑

The Astonishing Hypothesis: “You, your joys and your sorrows, your memories and your ambitions, your sense of personal identity and free will, are in fact no more than the behavior of a vast assembly of nerve cells and their associated molecules.”

Dualism: a common opinion

  • Descartes, “Are humans merely physical machines, merely physical things? No.” “Nonhuman animals are ‘Beast Machines’, merely robots; but people are different, we are immaterial souls that possess physical bodies.”
  • Humans are not limited to reflective actions, like machines; we are capable of coordinated creative spontaneous things. Language, etc.
  • One could doubt the existence of one’s body, like a madman might believe they have extra limbs. “How can I believe I am not crazy? How do I know I am not dreaming right now?” One cannot doubt that one is thinking.
  • “I knew that I was a substance, the whole essence or nature of which is to think, and that for its existence there is no need of any place, nor does it depend on any material thing. That is to say the soul by which I am, what I am, is entirely distinct from body.”
  • In our language, we talk about “my brain” as if we are somehow seperate from them.
  • Common sence indicates that somebody can be the same person even if their body has gone under radical changes.
  • Multiple personality disorder: perhaps many souls in one body and fighting for control?

Rebuttals:

  • Machines can actually play chess and do a range of complicated actions. Humans may just as well be physical machines.
  • You can observe brain activity which are somehow related to your thoughts.

neurons(神经元): basic component of brain; connected to thousands other neurons around it; are able to re-grow in parts of the brain; only 2 states: fired or not; there are ways to code intensity (different grades of feelings), number of neurons firing, frequency of firing, etc.

  • dendrites(树突): small tentacles on neurons; receives excitatory / inhibitory signals which increase / decrease the likelihood the neuron will fire;
  • axon(轴突): much longer than the dendrites; the neuron fires along the axon;
  • myelin sheath(髓鞘): surrounds the axon; insulation; helps firing work quicker;
  • synapse(突触): a tiny gap between a neuron’s axon and a nother neuron’s dendrite;

neuron types:

  • sensory neurons(感觉神经元): takes info from the world;
  • motor neurons(运动神经元): tell the muscles what to do;
  • interneurons(中间神经元): connects the two above (sensation and action); does the “thinking”;

neurotransmitters(神经递质): a chemical substance released by axons when a neuron fires; goes through the synapse and affects the dendrites;

psychopharmacology(精神药理学): tries controlling the neurotransmitters

  • agonists(兴奋剂): increases the effect of neurotransmitters either by making more or slowing the process of cleanup, or faking one (mimicking its results);
  • antagonists(抑制剂): decreases the effect of neurotransmitters by destroying, making it harder to produce more, or putting a “paste” on the dendrites which stops it connecting with the axon;
  • Curare(箭毒马鞍子): a particular type of antagonist; blocks motor neurons from affecting muscle fibers; causes paralyzation and stops breathing, causing eventual death;
  • alcohol is inhibitory; it inhibits the inhibitory neurotransmitters; large amounts of alcohol also inhibits the excitatory neurotransmitters, which causes you to pass out;
  • Amphetamine(安非他命): increase the amount of norepinephrine, which is responsible for general arousal;
  • Prozac(百忧解): makes serotonin(血清素) more prevalent, and in some extent helps alleviate depression;
  • L-DOPA(左旋多巴)：increases the supply of dopamine(多巴胺) and alleviates Parkinson’s disease(帕金森症) which involves destruction of motor control, caused by too little dopamine;

Brain versus computer:

  • the brain is a lot more damage resistant; on receiving a lot of brain damage you can still preserve some mental functions;
  • the brain is extremely fast; it works with tissue(组织) which is very slow;
  • the brain works through massively parallel distributed processing;
  • neural networks(神经网络): computational networks which looks like neurons; try to build smart machines by modeling them after brains;

You don’t need your brain for: newborn sucking; limbs withdrawing from pain; vomiting; raise your hand level with the ground, drop a book on it and it will return to the same height;
